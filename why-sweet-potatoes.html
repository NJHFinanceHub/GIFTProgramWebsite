The "Mother Teresa of Food"

Sweet potatoes are a nutritional powerhouse that can save lives and transform communities

A Global Crisis We Can Solve

Every year, preventable deaths occur due to nutritional deficiencies that sweet potatoes can address.

600,000

Children Die Annually

from Vitamin A deficiency - a preventable tragedy that sweet potatoes can help stop. Orange flesh sweet potatoes are recognized as a nutritional powerhouse that can prevent this.

1.5 Million

Children Die Annually

from diarrheal diseases - causing nearly 20% of all children's deaths, which is more than AIDS, malaria and measles combined. Sweet potatoes can help slow diarrhea.

That's 2.1 million preventable child deaths every year

The sad, tragic and devastating loss and pain for families that could be prevented annually

Nutritional Powerhouse

Sweet potatoes are packed with essential nutrients that combat malnutrition and disease.

Rich in Vitamin A

Orange-fleshed sweet potatoes provide more than 100% of daily Vitamin A needs, essential for vision, immune function, and child development.

Complex Carbohydrates

Provides sustained energy with a low glycemic index, perfect for maintaining stable blood sugar levels and preventing hunger.

Essential Minerals

High in potassium, fiber, and other essential nutrients that support overall health and prevent disease.

Both Roots AND Leaves Are Nutritious!

Sweet Potato Roots:

  • High in Vitamin A
  • Rich in carbohydrates
  • Good source of fiber
  • Contains Vitamin C

Sweet Potato Leaves:

  • High in protein
  • Rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Excellent source of iron
  • Contains antioxidants

Economic & Agricultural Benefits

Propagation Model

Sweet potatoes multiply rapidly through vine propagation. A single sweet potato can produce multiple vines, which can be cut and replanted to grow more plants.

This means:

  • Farmers don't need to buy seeds every season
  • One investment can provide food for years
  • Communities can share and expand production easily
  • Exponential growth creates food security

Diagram: Sweet potato vine propagation

Global Market Potential

#1 Crop

Described as the #1 crop to grow to help feed the planet by 2050

High-Profit

Markets and uses are growing rapidly worldwide

Versatile

Used in chips, fries, flour, sweeteners, and even car parts!

Sweet potatoes provide not just nutrition, but economic opportunity for farmers to generate income and lift themselves out of poverty.

Expanding Uses

Beyond being used as table potatoes and in pies, sweet potatoes have become popular as chips, fries, and flour. They're also becoming a sweetener replacement for corn syrup, and have even been used by Toyota to create plastic car parts!

Sustainability Advantages

Sweet potatoes are one of the most sustainable crops for addressing global food security.

Drought Resistant

Thrives in areas with limited water resources and can withstand dry conditions better than many other crops.

Grows in Poor Soil

Adaptable to marginal soils where other crops fail, making it ideal for impoverished regions.

Fast Growing

Matures in 3-4 months, allowing multiple harvests per year and quick returns for farmers.

Climate Resilient

Adapts well to changing climate conditions, making it a crucial crop for food security in the face of climate change.
