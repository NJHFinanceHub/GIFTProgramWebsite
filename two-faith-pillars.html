The Two Faith Pillars

GIFT and TRANSFORMATION - Foundational concepts that could compel us to live life differently

Living Grateful and Transformed

As you'll note, GIFT, the name of our ministry, is an acronym for Global Institute For Transformation, and we see these two faith pillars -- 1) GIFT, and 2) TRANSFORMATION -- as essential, foundational faith concepts.

Contemplating these two pillars could compel us to live life differently:

More Grateful, TRANSFORMED and transforming the world

(Living and loving more like Jesus and sharing selfless love to help those in need materially and spiritually)

PILLAR 1: GIFT

Reflecting on, appreciating and celebrating everything and everyone that God has freely given us as a GIFT

RECEIVE God's Many Gifts

Since everyone and everything are gifts from God, we should RECEIVE and more fully appreciate God's many gifts in humble appreciation. This involves:

  • Recognizing that our life, our loved ones, our abilities, and our opportunities are all gifts from God
  • Cultivating gratitude and thankfulness for all we have been given
  • Celebrating the blessings in our lives with joy and appreciation
  • Acknowledging God as the source of all good things

PILLAR 2: TRANSFORMATION

Allowing our God, who created us, to TRANSFORM our lives, so we can become more like Him; to live and love more like Jesus

RECIPROCATE to God

We RECIPROCATE back to God for all the wonderful gifts we've received from Him, and allow the Holy Spirit to TRANSFORM us so we can make our lives a GIFT of self back to God and to others -- just like Jesus. This involves:

  • Allowing the Holy Spirit to transform our minds to think, love and live more like Jesus
  • Becoming the face of Christ in our communities and across the world
  • Being open to God's transforming grace in our daily lives
  • Growing in holiness and becoming more Christ-like each day

REGIFT God's Love to Others

We REGIFT from the gifts we've received to help others in need -- spiritually or materially -- to transform their situation and transform the world with all the incredible love He's given us. This involves:

  • Sharing material blessings with those experiencing poverty and hunger
  • Sharing spiritual gifts through evangelization and discipleship
  • Transforming the world through acts of compassion, justice, and mercy
  • Making our lives a gift of selfless love to others

The Need to Affect Transformations is Critical to All Christians

United Methodist Church

"The Mission of the church is to make disciples of Jesus Christ for the TRANSFORMATION of the world."

USCCB Document on the Laity

"Co-Workers in the Vineyard of the Lord" states: "The risen Lord calls everyone to labor in a world that must be TRANSFORMED in view of the final coming of the Reign of God; the Holy Spirit empowers all with various GIFTS and ministries for the building up of the Body of Christ... All of the baptized are called to work toward the TRANSFORMATION of the world... All of the faithful are called in various ways to share in the Church's mission of announcing the reign of God and TRANSFORMING the world in the light of Christ."

Pope Francis

In his first Sunday Mass homily, Pope Francis indicated, "With Christ, we can TRANSFORM ourselves and the world."

Vatican Laudato Si Action Platform Published GIFT's Document

The Vatican Dicastery For Promoting Integral Human Development's Laudato Si Action Platform just published a GIFT document related to the two faith pillars of GIFT and TRANSFORMATION on its website as a "Resource" for their network of organizations concerned about the "Cry of the Poor" and the "Cry of the Earth."

Document Title:

"Overcoming the Post-Christmas Letdown - Via GIFT and TRANSFORMATION All Year Long"

This resource encourages focusing on these two faith pillars as a way of keeping the spirit, love and joy of Christmas alive all year long. It demonstrates how contemplating GIFT and TRANSFORMATION can help us live more grateful, transformed lives that actively transform the world.

Scripture Asserts The Pillars Of GIFT And TRANSFORMATION

These two pillars have surfaced in Scripture and also in multiple encyclicals and teachings produced by the Catholic Church.
