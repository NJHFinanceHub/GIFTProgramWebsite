About UNIF

The University of Fondwa isn't your typical university. Located in rural Haiti, UNIF focuses on practical education that directly addresses the needs of rural communities - agriculture, animal husbandry, community development, and sustainable business practices.

Their mission is straightforward but powerful: educate young Haitian men and women so they can create wealth in rural Haiti. Not just escape to the cities, but build thriving communities right where they are.

Students learn by doing - managing real farms, running actual businesses, solving genuine community challenges. When they graduate, they're not just ready for jobs; they're ready to create jobs and transform their communities.

Their Mission

To educate young Haitian men and women in order to create wealth in rural Haiti through practical, hands-on training in agriculture and community development.

GIFT's Partnership

GIFT provides sweet potato farming training as part of UNIF's agricultural curriculum, giving students practical skills in high-value crop production.

Sweet Potatoes in the Curriculum

At UNIF, sweet potato farming isn't just theory - it's a hands-on learning experience that prepares students for real agricultural work. Students manage actual sweet potato plots, learning everything from soil preparation to harvest and marketing.

Through GIFT's partnership, UNIF students learn why sweet potatoes are such a valuable crop for rural Haiti: high nutritional value, reliable yields, and good market prices. They learn the business side too - calculating costs, finding buyers, and turning crops into income.

Graduates take these skills back to their communities, teaching others and starting their own farming enterprises. It's education that creates ripple effects of prosperity.
