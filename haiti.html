Haiti Project

Bringing hope and nutrition to Haitian communities through sustainable sweet potato farming

Project Status: Active

Project Overview

Our Haiti project addresses extreme poverty and malnutrition through sustainable agriculture and church partnerships.

Location: Haiti

The Challenge

Haiti faces severe food insecurity, with over 4.9 million people experiencing hunger. Malnutrition rates are among the highest in the Western Hemisphere, particularly affecting children and pregnant women.

Our Solution

We partner with local churches to establish sweet potato farms that provide:

  • Nutritious food for vulnerable families
  • Income opportunities for local farmers
  • Agricultural training and expertise
  • Community development and empowerment

Success Stories

Marie's Story

Mother of three, Haiti

"Before this program, my children were often hungry. Now, we have sweet potatoes to eat every day, and I even sell some at the market. My youngest daughter's health has improved so much since we started eating the leaves in our meals. This project has given us hope."

Jean's Story

Farmer and trainer, Haiti

"I learned how to grow sweet potatoes through this program, and now I teach other farmers in my community. I've been able to feed my family and earn income by selling the harvest. The training gave me skills I never had before. I'm now helping five other families start their own sweet potato gardens."
