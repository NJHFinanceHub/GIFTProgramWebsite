Our Approach

Building sustainable partnerships that create lasting transformation through innovation and community

The Church Twinning Model

We connect churches across continents to create meaningful partnerships that transform communities.

U.S. Partner Church

  • Provides financial support and resources
  • Offers prayer and spiritual support
  • Shares expertise and agricultural knowledge
  • Connects through webcams and technology
  • Receives updates on project impact

International Partner Church

  • Manages local farming operations
  • Distributes produce to community members
  • Trains local farmers in best practices
  • Builds sustainable community programs
  • Shares stories and progress with partners

Rooted in Faith

Our work is grounded in Matthew 25 and the call to serve "the least of these."

"For I was hungry and you gave me something to eat, I was thirsty and you gave me something to drink, I was a stranger and you invited me in..."

- Matthew 25:35

Drawing from the USCCB Document on The Laity, we empower church communities to live out their faith through tangible service and partnership with those in need.

Technology Integration

We use cutting-edge technology to maximize agricultural outcomes and create transparency.

Soil Sensors

Real-time monitoring of soil moisture, pH levels, and nutrient content to optimize growing conditions and maximize yields.

Weather Monitoring

Local weather stations provide forecasts and climate data to help farmers plan planting, irrigation, and harvesting schedules.

Live Webcams

Partner churches can see their twin farms in real-time, creating connection and transparency across thousands of miles.

Poznan Supercomputing Center Partnership

We partner with the Poznan Supercomputing Center to analyze agricultural data, create predictive models, and optimize farming practices using advanced computational resources. This collaboration brings world-class scientific expertise to subsistence farmers.

USDA Expertise Partnership

We collaborate with U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) experts to bring cutting-edge agricultural science to our partner communities.

  • Access to research on sweet potato varieties and cultivation
  • Soil testing and nutrient management guidance
  • Pest and disease management strategies
  • Training materials adapted for local conditions
  • Post-harvest handling and storage best practices

Key Success Factors

  • Low-cost, low-tech base model accessible to all
  • Strong local church partnerships ensure sustainability
  • Sweet potato propagation eliminates ongoing seed costs
  • Technology enhances but doesn't replace human connection
  • Transparent reporting builds trust and accountability

Expand to 10 countries and partner with 100 churches within the next 5 years, impacting over 100,000 lives through sustainable sweet potato farming.
