About the Smallholder Farmers Alliance

With 7,200 farmer members operating 3,600 small farms (5 acres or less) across Haiti, the Smallholder Farmers Alliance (SFA) has created something remarkable - a system where farmers earn "tree currency" by planting and maintaining trees, then exchange those credits for seeds, tools, and agricultural training.

The SFA operates 33 tree nurseries clustered around 10 branch locations, ensuring every farmer member lives within walking distance of a nursery. This isn't just about trees - it's about rebuilding Haiti's decimated forests while simultaneously helping farmers increase their crop yields and household incomes.

The results speak for themselves: farmers see an average 40% increase in yields, and household incomes jump 50 to 100% depending on the region. When husband and wife farm together, they're recognized as separate members - acknowledging that women's contributions to farming are equal and essential.

GIFT's Partnership

GIFT collaborates with SFA to integrate sweet potato farming into their training programs, providing another high-value crop for farmers to grow alongside their reforestation work.

The Tree Currency System

1

Plant Trees

Farmers receive tree seedlings from nearby nurseries and plant them as orchards, living fences, or reforestation projects

2

Maintain Trees

Farmers care for their trees, ensuring they survive and thrive while preventing soil erosion

3

Earn Credits

Tree planting and maintenance earns "tree currency" - credits that have real value in the SFA system

4

Exchange for Resources

Credits are exchanged for quality seeds, tools, training, and agricultural services

Sweet Potatoes + Trees = Thriving Farms

The SFA's tree currency system and GIFT's sweet potato program work together beautifully. While farmers are planting trees to restore Haiti's forests and earning credits for agricultural inputs, they're also learning to grow one of the most valuable food crops available - sweet potatoes.

Sweet potatoes thrive in Haiti's climate and don't require expensive chemicals. They complement the SFA's philosophy perfectly: low-cost, sustainable practices that increase yields and income while protecting the environment.

Farmers can use their tree currency credits to access sweet potato vines and training, then turn around and sell the harvest for good prices at market. It's another tool in their toolbox for building prosperous, sustainable farms.
